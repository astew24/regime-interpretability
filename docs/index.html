Can a sparse model make market regime embeddings easier to explain?

This UC San Diego research repo trains autoencoders on rolling multi-asset return windows, then checks whether sparse features line up with external market indicators such as VIX, yield spread, momentum, and cross-asset correlation.

30 second version

What it studies

Rolling windows of ETF returns across equity, bond, commodity, volatility, and dollar-linked assets.

What it trains

A dense autoencoder first, then a sparse autoencoder on the frozen latent embeddings.

What it checks

Whether sparse features are easier to compare with known market indicators and baseline regime labels.

Model flow

1. DownloadPull daily market prices and indicator series with yfinance.
2. WindowCompute log returns and flatten standardized rolling windows.
3. CompressTrain a dense autoencoder to learn compact regime embeddings.
4. DecomposeTrain a sparse autoencoder with TopK activation on those embeddings.
5. InspectCompare features against indicators, HMM, K-means, ablations, and a simple backtest.

Honest limits

The labels are heuristic and the generated results should be rerun locally before being quoted. The backtest is a sanity check, not a trading strategy.
